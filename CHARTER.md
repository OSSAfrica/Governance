# OSSAfrica Governance Charter

## 1. Name

This charter governs the Open Source & Security Africa (OSSAfrica)
Governance Program.

## 2. Mission

OSSAfrica exists to strengthen open source collaboration and security
culture across Africa through transparent governance, inclusive community
participation, and sustainable leadership.

## 3. Objectives

The Governance Program will:

- Maintain a clear and transparent governance framework.
- Enable broad contributor participation and leadership pathways.
- Support working groups and chapters with clear operating guidance.
- Promote responsible security practices in community operations.
- Ensure decisions are documented and accountable.

## 4. Scope

This charter applies to:

- Governance documentation and policy lifecycle.
- Leadership accountability and role expectations.
- Working group and chapter governance operations.
- Community moderation and conflict-resolution governance paths.

Out of scope:

- Commercial product strategy.
- Vendor-specific control over OSSAfrica decisions.

## 5. Guiding Principles

OSSAfrica governance operates on these principles:

- Openness and transparent documentation.
- Inclusive participation and respectful collaboration.
- Merit-based trust earned through sustained contribution.
- Accountability for decisions and commitments.
- Security-conscious community operations.

## 6. Governance Bodies and Roles

### 6.1 OpenSSF BEAR Working Group

Provides strategic oversight and external governance alignment support.

### 6.2 OSSAfrica Special Interest Group (SIG)

Coordinates governance direction, priorities, and policy-level decisions.

### 6.3 Core Team

Executes day-to-day governance operations, implementation, and community
coordination.

### 6.4 Working Groups and Leads

Operate within approved charters and report progress using agreed cadence.

## 7. Membership and Participation

Participation is open to contributors who follow OSSAfrica policies and the
Code of Conduct.

Leadership roles require visible engagement, reliable delivery,
constructive collaboration, and adherence to governance standards.

## 8. Decision-Making Model

OSSAfrica follows a consensus-first model:

1. Proposals are documented through issues or pull requests.
2. Discussion is open for contributor and maintainer feedback.
3. Decisions seek rough consensus with documented rationale.
4. Unresolved governance disputes escalate to the OSSAfrica SIG.
5. Governance-level escalations may involve OpenSSF BEAR WG guidance.

Reference: [docs/decision-making-process.md](docs/decision-making-process.md)

## 9. Voting and Quorum

OSSAfrica is consensus-first. Voting is used when consensus cannot be reached.

- One vote per eligible governance participant in scope for the decision.
- Quorum requires at least 50% of eligible participants.
- A standard decision passes by simple majority of participants present,
  provided quorum is met.
- Electronic votes require a simple majority of all eligible participants.
- Charter amendments require a two-thirds majority.

If a vote remains unresolved, the matter may be escalated to the OSSAfrica SIG,
and when appropriate, to OpenSSF BEAR WG guidance.

## 10. Meetings and Reporting

- Core governance updates: weekly or bi-weekly as needed.
- Working group updates: monthly cadence.
- Roadmap and strategy reviews: quarterly.

Meeting outcomes should include documented decisions and action items.

Reference: [templates/meeting-notes-template.md](templates/meeting-notes-template.md)

## 11. Working Groups and Charters

All working groups should maintain an explicit charter defining purpose,
scope, deliverables, cadence, and review lifecycle.

Reference: [templates/charter-template.md](templates/charter-template.md)

## 12. Compliance With Policies

All participants must follow OSSAfrica governance policies and applicable Linux
Foundation/OpenSSF collaboration norms where relevant to joint governance work.

The governance process must remain open, transparent, non-discriminatory, and
professionally managed.

## 13. Antitrust and Meeting Conduct

Participants must comply with applicable competition/antitrust laws.

In governance meetings and calls, participants must not discuss competitively
sensitive matters such as pricing, market allocation, confidential business
strategy, bid coordination, or exclusionary coordination.

Meetings should:

- Follow prepared agendas.
- Capture minutes and decisions.
- Record and escalate objections to inappropriate discussion.

Reference: Linux Foundation Antitrust Policy [linuxfoundation.org/legal/antitrust-policy](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/legal/antitrust-policy)

## 14. Code of Conduct and Enforcement

All governance activities are subject to OSSAfrica conduct and moderation
policies.

- Code of conduct: [docs/code-of-conduct.md](docs/code-of-conduct.md)
- Moderation: [docs/moderation-guidelines.md](docs/moderation-guidelines.md)
- Conflict handling: [docs/conflict-resolution.md](docs/conflict-resolution.md)

## 15. Security and Confidentiality

Security-sensitive matters and vulnerability disclosure should follow the
repository security policy and approved escalation channels.

- Security policy: [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)
- Governance security notes: [docs/security.md](docs/security.md)

## 16. Community Assets and Control

Governance community assets should be held in organization-controlled accounts
or repositories (for example: source repositories, domains, and social channels)
to ensure continuity.

No individual maintainer should be a single point of control for critical
community assets.

## 17. Intellectual Property and Contribution Terms

- Code contributions are licensed under Apache-2.0 unless explicitly documented
  otherwise in repository policy.
- Documentation contributions are accepted under repository terms.
- All commits must include DCO sign-off in line with
  [developercertificate.org](https://developercertificate.org/).

## 18. Transparency and Records

Major governance decisions must be recorded in repository history via issues,
pull requests, and document updates.

## 19. Amendments

This charter may be amended through pull request with:

- Clear rationale and impact statement.
- Community review period.
- Maintainer/SIG approval and documented decision outcome.
- Two-thirds approval of eligible voting participants.

## 20. Review Cadence

This charter should be reviewed at least every 12 months, or sooner when
major governance changes occur.

## 21. Effective Date

Effective on merge to the `main` branch of the Governance repository.
