# **OSSAfrica Leadership Accountability Framework**

__Version 1.0__

## **1. Purpose**

This framework establishes non-negotiable expectations, responsibilities, and accountability standards for all OSSAfrica community leaders. Its goal is to ensure consistent engagement, transparent communication, shared responsibility, and high-quality stewardship of the community. Leadership is a commitment, not merely a title, and this framework serves as the foundation for maintaining the integrity and effectiveness of our leadership structure.

The framework applies to:

* Core Team Members
* Moderators
* Project Maintainers
* Working Group Leads
* Chapter Leads/Ambassadors
* Any person holding a leadership or representative role within OSSAfrica

## **2. Leadership Principles**

All OSSAfrica leaders are required to embody and actively demonstrate the following principles. These are not aspirational values—they are fundamental requirements of leadership within this community:

### **2.1. Visibility**

Leadership requires consistent, meaningful presence. Community members must *regularly see, hear from, and interact* with leaders. Absent or invisible leadership erodes trust and undermines community cohesion. Your presence is not optional—it is essential.

### **2.2. Reliability**

When leaders commit to a task, they deliver or update others proactively. Commitments are binding. Failure to deliver without adequate notice or communication is unacceptable and reflects poorly on both the individual and the community as a whole.

### **2.3. Transparency**

Information, decisions, and blockers must be communicated openly and early. Withholding information, making decisions in isolation, or failing to report challenges in a timely manner undermines collective progress and violates the trust placed in leadership positions.

### **2.4. Ownership**

Leaders take full responsibility for their areas—even during challenges—and proactively seek assistance when needed. Deflecting blame, abandoning responsibilities, or allowing tasks to fail silently is not acceptable. Ownership means being accountable for outcomes, not just intentions.

### **2.5. Respect & Professionalism**

Interactions must be welcoming, constructive, and strictly aligned with the OSSAfrica Code of Conduct. Leaders are held to a higher standard of conduct. Any behavior that falls short of professional excellence, including passive-aggressive communication, dismissiveness, or lack of respect, will be addressed immediately.

## **3. Expectations for All Leaders**

Leaders must meet the following baseline expectations without exception. These are minimum standards, not aspirational goals. Consistent failure to meet these expectations will result in reassessment of your leadership position:

### **3.1. Minimum Activity**

These activity standards are mandatory and non-negotiable:

* Participate in **weekly leadership check-ins** without fail
* Respond to leadership messages within **48 hours maximum**—silence is not acceptable
* Be visibly active in Discord at least **3 times per week** (messages, guidance, moderation, or contributions). Passive observation does not count as participation
* Contribute meaningfully to GitHub activity: issues, reviews, discussions, tasks. Your GitHub profile should reflect active leadership
* Participate in community calls, planning sessions, and public events. Repeated absences without valid reason will be escalated

### **3.2. Communication**

Clear, consistent communication is mandatory:

* Provide a weekly update without fail:

  * What you accomplished
  * What you plan next
  * Any blockers
* Notify the team in advance when unavailable for more than **5 days**. Last-minute notifications or prolonged silence without explanation will be treated as abandonment of responsibilities

### **3.3. Task Ownership**

Every leader must demonstrate clear ownership:

* Own at least one specific area (moderation, onboarding, community growth, events, documentation, etc.) and maintain it actively
* Deliver on assigned tasks within agreed timelines. Extensions require explicit approval and must be requested proactively, not after deadlines have passed
* Request support when facing obstacles. Struggling in silence and allowing tasks to fail is unacceptable and will be viewed as poor leadership judgment

### **3.4. Professional Conduct**

Leaders must exemplify the highest standards of behavior at all times, including:

* Respectful communication in all circumstances, even during disagreements
* Consistent, fair enforcement of the Code of Conduct without bias or favoritism
* Zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, or toxic behavior—leaders who fail to address such issues promptly are complicit
* Ensuring safe participation for all members. Creating or tolerating an unwelcoming environment is grounds for immediate removal from leadership

## **4. Leadership Roles & Responsibilities**

### **4.1. Core Team**

* Strategic decision-making
* Setting quarterly goals
* Ensuring all leaders remain active

* Running community-wide events

### **4.2. Moderators**

* Enforce rules and handle reports
* Start discussions and keep channels alive
* Help onboard new members

### **4.3. Project Maintainers**

* Manage GitHub repositories
* Review PRs on time
* Publish roadmaps & issues
* Mentor contributors and newcomers

### **4.4. Working Group Leads**

* Coordinate volunteers
* Publish goals & progress
* Run bi-weekly syncs
* Report to the Core Team

### **4.5. Ambassadors**

* Represent OSSAfrica externally
* Promote events, calls for contributions, and initiatives
* Connect communities, universities, and organizations

## **5. Accountability Framework**

Accountability is the cornerstone of effective leadership. This framework ensures that all leaders are evaluated fairly and consistently. Performance issues will be addressed directly and promptly.

### **5.1. Accountability Levels**

1. **Green (Active & Engaged)**

   * Meeting expectations consistently
   * Communicating regularly
   * Delivering on tasks
   * Participating in discussions and projects

2. **Yellow (Low Engagement)**

   * Inconsistent activity
   * Slow replies
   * Missed updates
   * Community noticing leadership silence

   **Action:**

   * Immediate direct check-in from Core Team
   * Clarify expectations and consequences
   * Identify blockers and establish support plan
   * 1–2 week improvement period with clear milestones
   * Failure to improve will result in progression to Red status

3. **Red (Inactive / Unresponsive)**

   * No visible activity for 2+ weeks without approved leave
   * No updates or communication despite multiple attempts at contact
   * Repeated missed responsibilities

   **Action:**

   * Immediate role reassessment
   * Temporary removal or reassignment—this is not optional
   * Replacement process initiated immediately to ensure continuity
   * Former leader may reapply for leadership positions after a minimum 90-day period and demonstrated renewed commitment

## **6. Processes**

### **6.1. Weekly Check-in Process**

Every leader must post a 3-point update by end of day each Monday (or first working day of the week):

1. **Last Week:** What I did (specific accomplishments, not vague statements)
2. **This Week:** What I'll do (concrete commitments)
3. **Blockers:** Anything stopping me (be specific and request help)

Missing check-ins without prior notice will be tracked and escalated after two consecutive misses.

### **6.2. Quarterly Planning**

* Quarterly roadmap created
* Tasks assigned and tracked
* Progress assessed using the green/yellow/red system

### **6.3. Leave of Absence**

Leaders may take breaks without penalty if:

* Notification is sent beforehand
* Duration is specified
* Handover notes are provided

### **6.4. Removal Process**

A leader will be removed from their role if:

* They remain in "Red" status for more than **3 weeks** without approved leave
* They violate the Code of Conduct in any manner
* They refuse to communicate or collaborate after direct intervention
* They demonstrate a pattern of unreliability that damages team trust

Removal is not punitive—it is a necessary action to protect community health, maintain standards, and ensure that leadership positions are held by those committed to the role. Removal decisions are final and will be communicated clearly to the affected individual and relevant community members.

## **7. Recognition & Motivation**

OSSAfrica recognizes active leaders through:

* Spotlight mentions
* Contributor badges
* Certificates of leadership
* Event shout-outs
* Priority opportunities (travel, speaking, collaborations)

## **8. Review & Updates**

This framework will be reviewed every 6 months by the Core Team and updated based on community feedback. However, the core principles of accountability, transparency, and active engagement are non-negotiable and will remain foundational to OSSAfrica leadership.

## **9. Acceptance of Leadership Responsibilities**

By accepting a leadership position within OSSAfrica, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to uphold this framework in its entirety. Leadership is earned through consistent action and maintained through sustained commitment. This community depends on your dedication—honor that responsibility.
