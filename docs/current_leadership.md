# Current Leadership

## Overview

OSSAfrica's leadership structure is built on specialized roles, each with distinct responsibilities within the open-source community ecosystem. These leaders drive strategic initiatives, maintain operational excellence, and steward community growth. All roles operate under the Leadership Accountability Framework and are expected to exemplify its principles.

---

## Leadership Team & Role Definitions

### **Community Partnerships Lead**

**Purpose:** Build and nurture strategic relationships that expand OSSAfrica's reach, credibility, and impact in the open-source ecosystem.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **External Engagement:** Identify, cultivate, and maintain relationships with organizations, universities, tech communities, and open-source foundations that align with OSSAfrica's mission
- **Collaboration**: Facilitate partnerships that create mutual value-including speaking opportunities, co-hosted events, joint initiatives, and knowledge exchange
- **Opportunity Sourcing:** Scout and evaluate partnership opportunities that can benefit community members (grants, internships, conferences, mentorships)
- **Strategic Alignment:** Ensure all partnerships reinforce OSSAfrica's values and advance our long-term community goals
- **Documentation:** Maintain records of active partnerships, contact details, and relationship status for organizational continuity

**Open-Source Context:**
In open-source communities, partnership leads serve as ambassadors and bridge-builders. They position the community as a credible player in the broader ecosystem and create pathways for members to grow beyond the community itself.

---

### **Design Lead**

**Purpose:** Establish and maintain a cohesive visual and user experience identity that reflects OSSAfrica's values and supports community engagement.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Brand & Visual Systems:** Develop and maintain design guidelines, brand assets, color palettes, typography, and visual standards across all platforms
- **UX/UI Design:** Lead design of community-facing platforms (website, documentation, dashboards, collaboration tools) ensuring accessibility and usability
- **Creative Direction:** Oversee design for marketing materials, event collateral, social media, and community resources
- **Design Community:** Build a design contributor community within OSSAfrica, mentoring designers and creating opportunities for design contributions
- **Accessibility:** Ensure all design decisions meet WCAG accessibility standards and are inclusive for diverse community members

**Open-Source Context:**
Design leads in open-source ensure that the community's digital spaces are welcoming, professional, and functional. They make the community's identity visible and craft experiences that encourage participation.

---

### **Programs Management Lead**

**Purpose:** Plan, coordinate, and execute community initiatives and programs that achieve strategic goals and maximize member engagement.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Program Planning:** Design and structure major community programs (bootcamps, mentorship tracks, hackathons, learning cohorts, etc.)
- **Timeline & Delivery:** Create detailed project plans, timelines, and success metrics for each program; ensure on-time, quality delivery
- **Resource Coordination:** Allocate budget, volunteers, tools, and materials across programs
- **Stakeholder Management:** Liaise with leadership, sponsors, volunteers, and participants to ensure alignment and communication
- **Impact Measurement:** Track program outcomes, gather feedback, and document lessons learned for continuous improvement
- **Documentation:** Maintain program documentation, guidelines, and runbooks for replicability

**Open-Source Context:**
Programs management leads orchestrate the structured initiatives that help open-source communities grow, learn, and connect. They transform strategy into executable programs that create tangible value for members.

---

### **AI/ML Lead**

**Purpose:** Guide OSSAfrica's exploration and adoption of AI/ML technologies while ensuring responsible, ethical, and community-focused implementation.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Technical Strategy:** Assess AI/ML opportunities relevant to OSSAfrica's mission and community needs
- **Capability Building:** Organize learning resources, workshops, and mentorship for members interested in AI/ML
- **Project Oversight:** Lead or oversee AI/ML initiatives and projects that the community undertakes
- **Ethical Governance:** Advocate for responsible AI practices, including bias mitigation, transparency, and ethical deployment
- **Ecosystem Awareness:** Monitor AI/ML trends in open-source and bring relevant knowledge back to the community
- **Collaboration:** Partner with AI/ML projects, organizations, and researchers to create opportunities for community members

**Open-Source Context:**
AI/ML leads position the community at the forefront of emerging technology trends. They ensure the community builds relevant technical skills while maintaining ethical standards-critical for responsible innovation in open-source.

---

### **Security Lead**

**Purpose:** Protect the community and its projects through proactive security practices, education, and vulnerability management.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Security Governance:** Establish and enforce security policies for OSSAfrica projects, infrastructure, and community platforms
- **Vulnerability Management:** Coordinate security audits, vulnerability disclosures, and remediation across OSSAfrica-maintained projects
- **Threat Awareness:** Monitor security threats, share threat intelligence with the community, and provide guidance on emerging risks
- **Security Education:** Deliver training, workshops, and resources to help developers and maintainers adopt secure coding practices
- **Incident Response:** Lead response to security incidents, ensuring transparency and swift resolution
- **Compliance & Standards:** Ensure OSSAfrica projects meet industry security standards and legal requirements

**Open-Source Context:**
Security leads are essential guardians of trust in open-source communities. They ensure that projects and infrastructure are protected against attacks, that community members understand security best practices, and that the community can confidently depend on OSSAfrica's platforms.

---

### **Communications Lead**

**Purpose:** Shape and amplify OSSAfrica's narrative, ensuring clear, consistent, and compelling communication across all channels.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Content Strategy:** Develop communication strategies that support community goals and messaging framework
- **Editorial Oversight:** Guide editorial direction for blogs, newsletters, social media, press releases, and community announcements
- **Content Production:** Create or coordinate creation of high-quality written, visual, and multimedia content
- **Messaging Consistency:** Ensure consistent tone, terminology, and messaging across all platforms and spokespersons
- **Stakeholder Communication:** Manage communication to key stakeholders (partners, sponsors, press, broader public)
- **Community Voice:** Amplify member stories, achievements, and contributions through storytelling and features
- **Crisis Communication:** Lead communication during incidents or challenges, maintaining transparency and community trust

**Open-Source Context:**
Communications leads ensure that open-source communities tell their story effectively. They build narrative around community achievements, maintain transparency during challenges, and help the world understand the community's value and mission.

---

### **Engineering Lead**

**Purpose:** Oversee the technical direction, quality, and infrastructure of OSSAfrica's projects and development initiatives.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Technical Architecture:** Define technical standards, architecture patterns, and best practices for OSSAfrica projects
- **Code Quality:** Establish code review standards, testing requirements, and quality assurance processes
- **Project Maintenance:** Oversee maintenance of key OSSAfrica projects, ensuring timely releases and updates
- **Contributor Mentorship:** Guide technical contributors, review contributions, and mentor aspiring developers
- **Infrastructure & DevOps:** Manage development infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and deployment systems
- **Technical Community Building:** Foster a culture of technical excellence and collaborative problem-solving
- **Roadmap Planning:** Contribute to technical roadmaps and prioritize development efforts based on community needs

**Open-Source Context:**
Engineering leads are the technical stewards of open-source projects. They ensure code quality, establish technical standards, mentor contributors, and make technical decisions that determine the sustainability and success of the community's software.

---

### **Finance Lead**

**Purpose:** Steward OSSAfrica's financial health with transparency, discipline, and alignment to community goals, ensuring resources are available and responsibly managed to support sustainable growth.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Financial Planning & Budgeting:** Develop, maintain, and regularly review annual and program-specific budgets in collaboration with other leads
- **Cashflow Management:** Track income and expenditure across all accounts, ensuring that OSSAfrica remains financially stable and obligations are met on time
- **Grant & Funding Management:** Support the identification, application, allocation, and reporting for grants, sponsorships, and donations
- **Financial Controls:** Define and enforce simple, clear financial policies (approval limits, expense categories, reimbursement rules) to prevent misuse of funds
- **Reporting & Transparency:** Prepare periodic financial reports for the leadership team and community, communicating how funds are obtained and used
- **Compliance:** Ensure that OSSAfrica's financial activities comply with any relevant legal, regulatory, and governance requirements
- **Documentation & Audit Readiness:** Maintain organized financial records (invoices, receipts, contracts, statements) to support audits, reporting, and leadership transitions

**Open-Source Context:**
In open-source communities, the finance lead safeguards trust by ensuring that money-often coming from sponsors, grants, or donations-is handled responsibly and transparently. This role makes it possible for the community to fund programs, infrastructure, and events in a way that is sustainable, auditable, and aligned with OSSAfrica's mission.

---

### **Operations Lead**

**Purpose:** Ensure smooth day-to-day operations, administrative efficiency, and organizational infrastructure that enables community functions.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Administrative Management:** Handle organizational logistics, scheduling, documentation, and record-keeping
- **Systems & Tools:** Manage community tools, platforms, databases, and systems (Discord, GitHub, project management tools, etc.)
- **Finance & Budget:** Oversee financial processes, budget allocation, expense tracking, and financial reporting
- **Process Development:** Design and implement operational processes that reduce friction and improve efficiency
- **Cross-Team Coordination:** Facilitate communication and collaboration between different leadership areas
- **Compliance & Governance:** Ensure adherence to legal, governance, and organizational policies
- **Continuity Planning:** Maintain institutional knowledge and create succession plans for critical functions

**Open-Source Context:**
Operations leads are the backbone of open-source community infrastructure. They ensure that the organizational plumbing works smoothly so that volunteers can focus on mission-critical work without getting bogged down in administrative chaos.

---

## Role Interdependencies

These roles form an integrated leadership system:

- **Engineering & Security** collaborate on secure code practices
- **Programs & Community Partnerships** jointly identify collaboration and program opportunities
- **Communications & all teams** coordinate messaging and community updates
- **Operations** supports all teams with infrastructure and administrative resources
- **Design** works across all teams to ensure consistent user experience
- **AI/ML & Engineering** partner on technical direction for emerging technologies

All roles are bound by the Leadership Accountability Framework and must demonstrate visibility, reliability, transparency, ownership, and professional conduct.
