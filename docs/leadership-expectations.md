# **OSSAfrica Leadership Expectations**

## **1. Purpose**

This document outlines the expectations, responsibilities, and professional standards required of all OSSAfrica leaders. It ensures a consistent level of accountability, reliability, and community stewardship across all leadership roles.

Leadership within OSSAfrica is a responsibility of service: supporting community members, sustaining momentum, and upholding the mission of strengthening open source and security awareness across Africa.

---

## **2. Core Leadership Principles**

### **2.1 Accountability**

Leaders are expected to honor commitments, meet deadlines, and transparently communicate progress or blockers. Ownership of tasks, decisions, and delegated responsibilities is essential.

### **2.2 Reliability**

Leaders should be consistently available and responsive within reasonable timeframes. Community members rely on leadership for guidance, structure, and follow-through.

### **2.3 Proactive Engagement**

Leaders initiate discussions, highlight gaps, propose improvements, and maintain visible activity across Discord, GitHub, and community spaces. Silence is interpreted as disengagement and affects team momentum.

### **2.4 Collaboration**

Leaders work constructively with other members, share context openly, and maintain a cooperative working environment. Coordination across SIGs, working groups, and the Core Team is expected.

### **2.5 Community Stewardship**

Leaders maintain a welcoming, inclusive, and constructive environment. They model professionalism and ensure community policies are followed.

---

## **3. Leadership Responsibilities**

### **3.1 Communication**

* Maintain active presence on Discord and GitHub.
* Respond to direct messages, mentions, and assigned issues within reasonable timelines (24–48 hours).
* Attend scheduled leadership meetings or provide updates if unavailable.

### **3.2 Execution and Delivery**

* Drive tasks within your area of responsibility to completion.
* Participate in roadmap execution and milestone planning.
* Provide weekly written check-ins and maintain transparency on status.

### **3.3 Community Engagement**

* Welcome new members and encourage active participation.
* Initiate discussions to keep channels active and relevant.
* Support community calls and facilitate knowledge-sharing or demos.

### **3.4 Governance Alignment**

* Follow OSSAfrica governance guidelines.
* Uphold open source best practices, including documentation, transparency, and merit-based decision-making.
* Coordinate with the Core Team to ensure alignment with the OpenSSF BEAR WG.

---

## **4. Performance Expectations**

### **4.1 Minimum Engagement**

Leaders are expected to maintain meaningful weekly participation. Extended periods of inactivity without notice undermine community trust and will lead to review of the role.

### **4.2 Deliverables**

Each leader must complete role-specific deliverables as defined during planning cycles and check-in sessions.

### **4.3 Professional Standards**

Leaders must conduct themselves respectfully and uphold OSSAfrica’s Code of Conduct at all times.

---

## **5. Accountability and Review Process**

### **5.1 Regular Check-ins**

All leaders must submit the Weekly Leadership Check-In report. This ensures shared visibility and prevents silent failure.

### **5.2 Follow-Up and Support**

If a leader is struggling, the Core Team will provide support, adjust scope, or redistribute responsibilities as appropriate.

### **5.3 Inactivity Protocol**

A leader may be transitioned out of a role if any of the following occur:

* Repeated missed check-ins without notice.
* Lack of visible contribution for more than three consecutive weeks.
* Failure to uphold community or governance standards.

### **5.4 Reinstatement**

Leaders stepping down or being cycled out may return to a role through the regular nomination and review process.

---

## **6. Leadership Commitment**

Taking a leadership position in OSSAfrica signifies agreement to:

* Serve the community with integrity.
* Prioritize transparency and communication.
* Uphold the mission and help drive sustained growth.
